🍇 Climbers




"climbers are plants that cannot stand upright on their own and grow by climbing on other plants, walls, fences, or other supports. They have long, flexible stems and use different methods to climb."


Some common types of climbers include:

  1. Tendril climbers – They use thin, coiling structures called tendrils to grip supports.
  2. Twining climbers – Their stems wrap around a support as they grow.
  3. Root climbers – They produce tiny aerial roots that attach to surfaces.


Characteristics of climbers🌿

  • Have weak, soft, or flexible stems.

  • Need support to grow upward.

  • Can cover walls, fences, trees, or trellises.

  • Often grown for their fruits, flowers, or decorative appearance.


Did you know? 💡

🌱 Some climbers can grow very quickly, adding several centimeters in a single day under good conditions.

🌀 Pea plants use tendrils that curl like springs to grab onto supports

🧗 English ivy can climb walls using tiny aerial roots that stick to surfaces.

🍇 Grapevine is a climber that produces delicious grapes.

🌸 Many climbers, such as Bougainvillea and Jasmine, are grown for their colorful flowers and sweet fragrance.
