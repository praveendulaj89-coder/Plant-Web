🌸 Flowering Plants




"Flowering plants are plants that produce flowers and form seeds enclosed within a fruit. They belong to a group called angiosperms, which are the largest and most diverse group of plants on Earth."


Characteristics of Flowering Plants

  • Produce flowers for reproduction.
  • Seeds develop inside an ovary, which later becomes a fruit.
  • Have specialized tissues (xylem and phloem) for transporting water and nutrients.
  • Can be found in almost every habitat, from deserts to rainforests.


Types of Flowering Plants

  1. Monocots – have one seed leaf (e.g., grasses, lilies, orchids).
  2. Dicots (Eudicots) – have two seed leaves (e.g., roses, beans, sunflowers).


Did you know? 💡

🌻 1. Sunflowers follow the Sun

Young sunflowers turn their faces to follow the Sun across the sky, a behavior called heliotropism. Mature flowers usually end up facing east.

🌺 2. Some flowers smell like rotten meat

The giant Corpse flower produces a powerful odor similar to decaying flesh to attract flies and beetles for pollination.

🌷 3. Tulips were once worth a fortune

In the 1600s, rare tulip bulbs became so valuable in the Netherlands that a single bulb could cost as much as a house during "Tulip Mania."

🌸 4. Orchids are incredibly diverse

There are over 25,000 species of orchids, making them one of the largest families of flowering plants.

🌼 5. Flowers have secret UV patterns

Many flowers have ultraviolet markings that humans can't see, but bees can. These patterns act like landing guides to help pollinators find nectar.
