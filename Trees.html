🌳 Trees




"A tree is a large, long-lived, woody perennial plant that typically has a single main stem, called a trunk, supporting a crown of branches and leaves. Trees belong to many different plant families and are found in nearly every terrestrial ecosystem, from tropical rainforests to temperate woodlands and boreal forests. They are among the largest and longest-living organisms on Earth, with some species living for thousands of years."


Characteristics of trees🌳

Trees generally share the following features:

  • Woody stem (trunk): The trunk is composed of strong, woody tissue that supports the tree's weight and transports water and nutrients.

  • Roots: A tree's root system anchors it firmly in the soil, absorbs water and minerals, stores food, and helps prevent soil erosion.

  • Branches and crown: Branches spread outward from the trunk to form the crown, which supports the leaves, flowers, and fruits.

  • Leaves or needles: Leaves perform photosynthesis, converting sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water into food (glucose) while releasing oxygen. Some trees, such as pines, have needle-like leaves.

  • Growth: Trees grow in height and thickness through specialized tissues called meristems. In most woody trees, annual growth produces rings that can be used to estimate age.

  • Longevity: Most trees live for decades or centuries, while certain species can survive for thousands of years.


Main parts of a tree🌳

  1. Roots

    • Absorb water and minerals from the soil.
    • Anchor the tree.
    • Store food reserves.

  2. Trunk

    • Provides structural support
    • Contains tissues that transport water, minerals, and food.
    • Protects the inner tissues with bark.

  3. VBark

    • Covers and protects the trunk from pests, diseases, and physical damage.
    • Reduces water loss.

  4. Branches

    • Support leaves, flowers, and fruits.
    • Position leaves to receive maximum sunlight.

  5. Leaves

    • Carry out photosynthesis.
    • Exchange gases through tiny openings called stomata.
    • Regulate water loss through transpiration.

  6. Flowers, fruits, and seeds

    • Flowers are the reproductive structures of flowering trees.
    • Fruits protect seeds and help disperse them.
    • Seeds grow into new trees under suitable conditions.


Did you know? 💡

1. Trees communicate. They can send chemical signals through their roots and are often connected by underground fungi, allowing them to share nutrients and warn nearby trees of pests.

2. The tallest known tree in the world is Hyperion, a Coast redwood in Redwood National Park, standing over 116 meters (380 feet) tall.

3. The oldest known individual tree is a Great Basin bristlecone pine nicknamed "Methuselah," which is more than 4,800 years old.

4.A single large tree can produce enough oxygen each year for several people, although the exact number varies depending on the tree species, size, and growing conditions.

5.Trees absorb carbon dioxide (CO₂) from the atmosphere and store carbon in their trunks, branches, roots, and leaves, helping reduce greenhouse gases.
